My current position specifically works to supply remote technical support for clients, as well as logging inquiries and documentation through our internal problem management database, maintaining knowledge of company products and customer service processes, and aiding in any other technical roles as needed.

With so many tasks for me to eventually take on, I am positive I will have an entirely new perspective on IT within the first month.


Clerical Student Assistant & Peer Learning Assistant - University of Missouri - 2019-2022

- The University of Missouri-Columbia, founded in 1839, is Missouri's largest university and the flagship of the four-campus University of Missouri System. It's a public land- grant research university in Columbia, Missouri, Mizzou is home to over 30,000 students. As one of them, I of course ended up working on-campus for most of my time there. My position was held in two main locations, Ellis Library and various residential halls, and my time was spent mainly working to allocate merchandise for the appropriate clientele, utilize administrative databases to manage data for students and buildings, greet guests, and direct inquiries to designated departments. As a Peer Learning Assistant (PLA), I worked remotely to facilitate a university's IT course, "Game Theory and Design", as an undergraduate teacher's assistant. In doing so, I graded assignments, answered emails, held office hours for 80+ students, and troubleshooted students' issues with in-class software: Epic Games Unreal Engine.

My position was not one either. Despite that, Hibachi seemed to be a hub for many different people, all with only one thing in common: their love for that god- forsaken restaurant. I'm glad I quit.


Cashier & Food Runner - McDonald's - 2016-2017

- I don't think this needs any introduction, but McDonald's Corporation is an American- based but international fast food chain. Founded in 1940 in California, McDonald's has become a household menu all across the globe, featuring everything from breakfast items like McMuffins and McGriddles to lunch items like Big Macs and Quarter Pounders to staples like fries and ice cream. My position as a cashier and occasional food runner was only a cog in the McDonald's machine, necessary to provide a high level of customer service during high-volume operations, mastery of the point-of-service computer system for taking orders, and handling transactions exchanges efficiently and accurately.
- Funnily enough, I believe that my experience at McDonald's was one that really shaped me as a person. Not necessarily a great experience, I came out of it with the firm belief that everyone should hold a food-service/retail position at least once in their lives. I still stand by that today.
